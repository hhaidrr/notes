# Growing on Twitter:

## Getting Impressions
This is based on the Twitter algorithm and whether it decides to show your tweet to people or not.
The algorithm has the primary objective of keeping users on the platform for as long as possible.
This means serving them content they are likely to engage with and be interested in. Keeping them scrolling.
To get more impressions, you need to make your tweets more engaging and interesting to your target audience.

For example I tweeted about Gemini Nano Banana and Grok imagine at a time when they were trending. I saw good impressions on those tweets.
The following day I posted around the typical engagement window of around 9am. I expected good impressions but the tweet got very low impressions (single digits).
This seemed odd. Then I learnt that what I had tweeted about (Google stitch) was not trending.
So the algorithm did not push it out to many people. 
Only my followers saw it if they open their app during that time.

So when trying to optimize for impressions, you need to consider:

1. Content Relevance and Timeliness:
    - Whats trending right now?
        The algorithm heavily prioritizes content related to current events and trending topics.
        when you post on a trending topic, the algorithm has a large, established audience to show it to, which can
        lead to a massive boost in impressions beyond your follower count.
    - What's Niche? 
        Content on a non-trending, niche topic like "Google Stitch" will get a much smaller push from the algorithm. 
        Impressions for these posts will rely primarily on your existing followers.

2. Initial Engagement:
    - The "Momentum" Effect.
        When a tweet is posted, the algorithm shows it to a small sample of people first (often a mix of followers and non-followers).

    - The Feedback Loop: 
        If this initial group engages with the tweet (likes, replies, retweets),
        it sends a strong signal to the algorithm that the post is valuable.
        The algorithm will then "boost" the tweet and show it to a progressively larger audience, leading to a viral effect.
        Without this initial engagement, the tweet's reach is capped.

3. The Role of Content Format
    - Rich Media is Key:
        Tweets with visual content (images, GIFs, videos) are given a boost by the algorithm because they are more 
        likely to grab a user's attention and increase engagement. This is especially true for native video content (videos uploaded directly to the platform).

    - Threads and Polls:
        These formats are designed to keep users on the platform longer. 
        A compelling thread encourages users to read through a series of connected tweets,
        while a poll requires a click, both of which are positive signals to the algorithm.


4. Community and User Behavior
    - Your Network Matters:
        Impressions are not just about what you post,
        but also about who you and your followers interact with.
        The algorithm is more likely to show your tweets to people who have a history of engaging with you or with accounts similar to yours.

    - Active Engagement:
        Simply posting good content is not enough.
        You must also actively engage with others. Replying to comments, liking and retweeting other people's posts,
        and participating in conversations signals to the algorithm that you are a valuable and active member of the community.

In Summary: The Core Principles
    To maximize impressions, don't just post content; participate in the platform's conversation. Your impressions will be highest when you create high-quality, relevant content that the algorithm wants to promote because it is either:

    Highly Timely: You are part of a trending conversation with a large, active audience.

    Highly Engaging: Your content format and initial reception from a small audience tell the algorithm that it's worth showing to many more people.
