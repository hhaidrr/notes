# Networking - Professional Relationships

This is a methodology for bare-minimum (bootstrapped) networking.

Simply utilizing past work colleagues of roughly the same level of seniority that you've established 
decent rapport with.

Reaching out to them offering part-time contribution to the project/organization for a partnership agreement.

We will have to establish a fair yet competitive agreement for their time and effort.

The fact that they are contributing early, they get foundership status and a share of the company.

Be transparent, honest, and open about the project and the vision. Set clear expectations and goals.
Set clear incentives and what it will be like, but tell them why they would be doing it.
To have better control on their vision while working with other engineers to achieve their vision at scale.

I am interested in gathering a group of engineers to create good products that are useful. This may be AI today, or quantum developer tools tomorrow, or flying cars the day after. The point is to excercise our engineering skills and execute on what we think the next abstractions will be.


At most their should be a limit of how many founders we can have so that we can keep the company productive and manage overhead from conflict of decisions.

# What would I actually write to them.
Subject: An Exciting Opportunity to Build Something Great Together

Hey [Colleague’s Name],

I hope you’re doing well! It’s been a while since we worked together, but I’ve always admired your skills and the way you approach engineering challenges.
I wanted to reach out because I’m starting something that I believe has the potential to grow into something truly impactful, and I’d love for you to be a part of it.

Right now, I’m working on [briefly describe the project].
It’s still in its early stages, but the vision goes far beyond just this project.
The long-term goal is to bring together a team of engineers who share a passion for building innovative, high-quality products—and eventually, to establish an organization that fosters engineering excellence and creativity.

To start, I’m looking for someone to collaborate with part-time, helping shape both the technical and strategic direction of what we’re building.
 But more than that, I want this to be the foundation of something bigger—an opportunity to create an engineering-driven company where we build things the right way and work on projects that truly excite us.


I know you have a lot on your plate, but if this sounds even remotely interesting, I’d love to chat and see if it’s something that aligns with your interests
 Let me know when you’re free, and we can set up a time to talk


Looking forward to catching up!

Best,
[Your Name]
