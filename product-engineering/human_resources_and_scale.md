# Human Resources and Scale

Producing output is scaled in only one of two ways:

* Making the producer more efficient.
* Making more producers.

This is true in everything from people to computers.

## Reflection for the Recluse
As I write this. I reflect on the fact that I like to do much on my own. I rarely ask for
help. I like knowing how to do something myself so I maintain directional control over
the end result. I prefer not to deal with alignment issues, people issues.

This is fine, as an individual working on producing on their own. This individual
prioritizes their autonomy, control, and product visibility. However, this 
individual is naive if they think they will simultaneously aquire a large ceiling to scale.
They can become as efficient as possible. However, it simply can't compete with parallelism
done well.

## Parallelism
Predicates:

* Parallelism is ultimately valuable since it gives us something that is not physically
possible otherwise. Concurrency can be good, but never match parallelism.

* There is no machine on earth that currently replicates the utility of a human resource.
Else there would be no human product engineers or any other career for that matter. 
AGI still hasnt come to deliver its threat.

If these previous predicates are true. Then we can therefore state that active working 
human resources in parallel provides a un-replicable output. Thus proving the unique
value of the *organization*.

## Organizations
Organizations is a vast topic since there are many aspects to one, and many ways to 
operate each one of these aspects on their own.

Working with others scales things. Good and bad. It scales:

* Knowledge
* Skills
* Experience
* Gross Productivity

These are all good things, however it comes with scaling some of the bad:

* Directional Confliction
* Communication Overhead

The good things are self-explanatory, but the bad things can be talked about in depth.

## Directional Confliction

Directional confliction arrives because of the simple fact that everybody perceives
differently. Thus they think and act differently as their output.

Product engineers will disagree. Every department will have disagreements. Shareholders
will disagree. If everyone becomes the captain of the ship. This ship will go nowhere. 
At least not fast.

Always maintain a head-count limit on people who influence the direction of the product.
Direction is fundamentally uniform. You cannot chose two directions. Else they will be 
fighting each other. Slowing down the reach of either direction. Scale this, and slow 
things down even more. This is one of the things you don't want to scale.

The group of product engineers should be big enough to self correct mistakes, but 
small enough to navigate disagreements quickly. Before even appending a new product 
engineer, the existing ones must vet how much they align with the existing direction. 
We not only want to ensure that disagreements that come up are navigated quickly, but 
that they are small.

Disagreements between this small group cannot really be controlled in any way outside of
personal conduct. It is naive to think so. It will be up to human nature. 

However the control comes in limiting how many people are allowed in. This not only
only includes technical people. It includes investors, sales people, engineers, everyone 
that has power to push the direction of the product to a certain capacity.
