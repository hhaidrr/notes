# Initial Users
Having strategies for getting the rubber to the road and exposing a product idea to initial users is a critical objective
during the startup phase for idea validation and initial clients. 

# B2C Strategies
1. Incentivized referral, optionally even pay them to be users
- Dropbox, PayPal
2. Make posts on existing platforms where potential target users are already active
- Airbnb
3. Target market influencers to promote the product and host exclusive events, sponserships (youtube channels)
- Uber
4. Create a landing page explaining the product. User's can sign up for more details and better offering. Gather initial demand validation
- Buffer
5. Invite from personal trusted network to provide valuable feedback and referrals.
- Slack
6. Free initial installs that don't require user action. Opt them in as users instead
- Stripe
7. In person booths, hosting parties, people can interactively demo the product.
- Tinder

# B2B Strategies
1. In person booths at industry-conventions, expos, trade shows, people can interactively demo the product.
- Box
2. Contact existing teams, and decision makers in your personal professional network
- InVision
3. Start with SMBs before targeting enterprise
- Salesforce
4. Partner with complementary products or services. Bundle and co-market your solution with theirs.
- Slack
5. Cold outreach, identify key decision makers at target companies, craft highly personalized cold emails that address their pain points
- Zenifits (used targeted cold emails to connect with HR departments at SMBs)
6. Write blog posts and white papers, requiring users emails to download the content
- Intercom
7. Engage in forums like Reddit and niche Slack/linkedin groups to connect with professionals who could use their product.
- Notion (offer valuable insights and solutions rather than directly selling, position yourself as a helpful expert in the community)
8. Partner with well known companies in the industry 
- Snowflake
9. Join incubators or accelerators to connect with early clients and mentors
- Segment

# The Fortune 5 Million
From a talk by DHH: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0CDXJ6bMkMY 

Selling direct to individual consumers is very challenging, and selling to enterprise is challenging,
selling to SMBs of 50+ employees is also challenging, but there are companies in the fortune 5 million.
Companies like 2-3 people or 5 people. Very very small companies, but they are willing to pay if you 
can solve a problem they're experiencing. Selling is challenging all around, but the fortune 5 million 
is a good entry point.


Leverage existing platforms or communities (e.g., Craigslist, forums).
Offer value with minimal risk (e.g., free trials, freemium models).
Incentivize users to refer others (e.g., Dropbox, PayPal).
Start small and iterate based on feedback (e.g., Slack, Buffer).
Focus on specific demographics or niches (e.g., Tinder, Stripe).

# Actionable components
Launch activity + Demoable Idea

Launch activity = Any of the strategies mentioned above
Demoable Idea = Any of (Landing page, Functional Interactive Application)
