# Surveying what the market needs

This document should result in an actionable plan that can be easily carried out within a week of setup that allows us 
to emperically develop a mental model and feel for real market and user needs.

The thought comes from what we have seen when we go through job interviews. After a recurring amount of interviews, we start to develop a mental model and feel for 
the *job* market needs, this is *one* market, interviews are in a concrete strategy of trying to propose a solution (ourself) to a problem (the job).
The outcome of this, even if we dont get any job, we get information, interviews are a series of experiments with outcomes, a series of experiments gives us 
an understanding of what the market is looking for.

We see that *interviews* are a concrete way of developing a model of demand for the *job* market. So if we abstract this a bit, and apply a similar strategy to other markets, we can start to develop a model of demand for other markets of product and services.

The goal of this document is to develop a strategy that allows us to survey the market needs for a specific product or service through real actions for emperical results.

## What can we produce as a sole individual?
Without the development of the network principle, we are currently by ourselves, however, this is an implementation detail when it comes to the outcome of successful products. Users don't care who or how many people were involved with making a product, so long as the product satisfies them.

We as an individual can produce:
- dumb hardware (purely mechanical products)
e.g. those plastic ring things that allow you to hold your phone from the back,
any mechanical product that is not electronic. 
They go viral and become successful.
Only requires us to know how to send instructions to a manufacturer. Then handle the logistics of shipping and selling.

- smart hardware (devices)
e.g. smart home devices, smart watches, smart glasses, etc.
This requires use to know how to write firmware, print pcb's and assemble the devices.
This is probably the most complex product we can produce as a sole individual since it requires deep hardware knowledge and software knowledge.

- smarts (software)
e.g. web apps, mobile apps, etc. This is the most common product we can produce as a sole individual since we are a software engineer and it is in our realm of doing.

So realistically for the near term, we should aim to produce dumb hardware and software.

## What are the equivalent of interviews in other markets?
ChatGPT response:

1. Cold Outreach (B2B / Freelance Services)
Analogy: Like sending your résumé to companies, but with a pitch instead of a CV.

Action: Email or DM potential customers with a specific problem/solution combo.

Feedback: Do they respond? Are they curious? What objections do they raise?

2. Landing Page with Ads (Product Market)
Analogy: Like going to an interview with a specific offer and seeing who shows up.

Action: Create a one-page website pitching a product or service. Run $50 in targeted ads.

Feedback: Measure clicks, time on page, and email sign-ups (as a proxy for intent).

3. Gumroad / Etsy / Product Hunt Listing (Consumer Digital Product)
Analogy: Listing yourself on job boards.

Action: Create a product idea and list it for sale, even as a pre-order.

Feedback: Do people visit? Do they purchase or wishlist? What comments come in?

4. User Interviews via Calendly + Reddit / Discord / Twitter
Analogy: Informational interviews or pre-screenings.

Action: Post in niche communities offering 15-min calls for feedback or discussion on a problem.

Feedback: Who signs up? What pain points are revealed?

5. Freelancer Platform Proposal Submissions (e.g., Upwork)
Analogy: Very literal interview equivalent.

Action: Submit proposals with custom pitches for real client projects.

Feedback: Response rate, questions asked, whether you're shortlisted.

6. Pre-sales via Chat in Niche Forums
Analogy: Casual coffee chats with hiring managers.

Action: Join Discords or forums, offer a solution (e.g. "I help X do Y. Anyone struggling with that?").

Feedback: Engagement, private messages, critique.

7. Open Source with a Monetization Hook
Analogy: Showcasing a portfolio that solves real problems.

Action: Release a useful tool/library with a CTA like "want help customizing? email me".

Feedback: GitHub stars, issues, support requests.

## Action plan
chatgpt response:
🎯 Goal
Within one week, run a set of small, real-world experiments that generate qualitative and quantitative signals of demand for a product or service idea.

🗓️ 7-Day Market Demand Discovery Plan
Day 0 – Setup (1–2 hours)
Pick a niche/problem area you're interested in solving.

Examples: productivity for devs, lightweight analytics, AI tutoring, financial dashboards.

Define a 1-line value prop: “I help [target group] [solve problem] with [approach/tool].”

Set up tracking:

Google Sheet or Notion to log outreach, sign-ups, responses, and takeaways.

Create a throwaway domain or Substack page if needed for landing page tests.

Day 1 – Cold Outreach (10 targets)
Find 10 potential users on:

LinkedIn, Twitter, Discord, IndieHackers, Reddit, Slack communities.

DM/email them:

Brief message: “Hey, I help [X] solve [Y]. I’m running short calls to learn more. Want to chat?”

Track:

Open/read response rate

Call sign-ups

Pain points mentioned

Day 2 – Landing Page Test
Create a simple landing page (use Carrd, Typedream, or Notion).

Include: headline, problem, your offer, email capture or Calendly.

Run ads (~$20) or post organically on:

Reddit (e.g., r/startups, r/SideProject)

Twitter

Hacker News "Show HN" or IndieHackers

Track:

Clicks, time on page, sign-ups

Day 3 – Live Offer in Communities
Join niche Discord servers, subreddits, Facebook groups.

Post a low-key offer:

“I’ve helped 2 people automate this X process — looking for others who need it.”

Track:

Comments, interest, follow-ups

Feedback about the offer language

Day 4 – Apply to 5 Relevant Upwork/Fiverr Jobs
Submit 5 personalized proposals for jobs that match your idea/problem space.

Track:

View count, responses, shortlists

What the clients ask for in replies

Day 5 – Run 2–3 User Calls
From earlier outreach, run 2–3 short user interviews.

Focus: “What are you struggling with? How do you solve it now?”

Ask:

Would they pay for a better way?

What does success look like?

Day 6 – Share a Freebie or Tool
Build or offer a minimal free asset:

Template, calculator, script, checklist.

Post it in relevant places:

e.g., “I made a checklist for devs to optimize their Stripe integration. Free here.”

Add CTA: “Need custom help? DM me.”

Day 7 – Synthesize & Decide
Review all tracked signals:

Response rates

Recurring problems

Words users use to describe pain

What people were willing to do (calls, emails, downloads)

Decide:

Is this a strong signal to keep going?

Or pivot to a new hypothesis?

📊 Metrics to Track
Channel	Metric
Cold outreach	% replied, % booked call
Landing page	CTR, bounce rate, email sign-ups
Communities	Comments, interest, PMs
Freelance sites	Proposals viewed, % replied
Interviews	Common problems, willingness to pay
