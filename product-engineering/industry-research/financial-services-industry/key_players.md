## Infrastructure

### 1. Core Banking and Financial Systems

These are the backbone of traditional finance and are critical for processing transactions, managing accounts, and maintaining financial records.

Core Banking Systems   

- Software platforms used by banks to manage customer accounts, process payments, and facilitate loans.
- Examples: Temenos, Finacle by Infosys, FIS, Oracle FLEXCUBE.

Payment Processing Networks  

- Card Networks: Visa, Mastercard, American Express, Discover, etc.
- ACH (Automated Clearing House): Networks like Nacha in the U.S. for batch processing of bank transfers.
- Real-Time Payment Systems: FedNow (U.S.), Faster Payments (U.K.), UPI (India).

Financial Market Infrastructure (FMI):  

- Stock exchanges (e.g., NYSE, NASDAQ).
- Clearinghouses (e.g., DTCC, LCH).
- Central Counterparties (CCPs) for derivatives and securities.

### 2. Payment Gateways and Processors

Facilitators of electronic payments for e-commerce, fintech, and traditional financial services.

Payment Gateways: Secure interfaces that connect merchants with payment networks.  

- Examples: PayPal, Stripe, Adyen, Square.

Payment Processors: Handle the technical routing of payment information.  

- Examples: Worldpay, Fiserv, Global Payments.

Mobile Payment Platforms: Enable payments via smartphones.  

- Examples: Apple Pay, Google Pay, Samsung Pay.

### 3. Communication and Data Exchange Networks

Enable secure and efficient information exchange between financial institutions.

SWIFT (Society for Worldwide Interbank Financial Telecommunication):  

- A global network for cross-border financial messaging.
- Used by banks and institutions for international money transfers.

SEPA (Single Euro Payments Area):  

- A framework for streamlined payments across Europe.

APIs (Application Programming Interfaces):  

- Allow seamless integration between financial services and third-party applications.
- Examples: Plaid (U.S.), Tink (Europe).

### 4. Blockchain and Distributed Ledger Technology (DLT)

Emerging infrastructure for decentralized finance (DeFi) and cryptocurrency.

Blockchain Networks:  

- Public Blockchains: Bitcoin, Ethereum.
- Private Blockchains: Hyperledger, Quorum.

Decentralized Finance (DeFi): Platforms that provide financial services without intermediaries.  

- Examples: Uniswap, Aave, MakerDAO.

Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDCs): Blockchain-based national currencies under development by governments.

### 5. Risk Management and Security Infrastructure
Protects financial systems against fraud, cyberattacks, and operational risks.

Fraud Detection Systems:  

- Machine learning-based tools for real-time fraud analysis.
- Examples: FICO Falcon, Sift.

Encryption and Secure Messaging:  

- TLS, SSL for data in transit.
- PKI (Public Key Infrastructure) for authentication.

Identity Verification and KYC (Know Your Customer):  

- Services for onboarding and compliance.
- Examples: Onfido, Jumio, Trulioo.

### 6. Regulatory and Compliance Infrastructure
Ensures adherence to legal and financial regulations.

Regulatory Technology (RegTech):  

- Solutions for monitoring compliance and reporting requirements.
- Examples: ComplyAdvantage, Elliptic, MetricStream.

AML (Anti-Money Laundering) and Sanctions Screening:  

- Software for detecting and preventing illicit financial activity.
- Examples: NICE Actimize, Dow Jones Risk & Compliance.

### 7. Specialized Fintech Infrastructure
Tailored for new-age financial services.

Embedded Finance Platforms:  

- Enable non-financial companies to integrate financial services.
- Examples: Stripe Treasury, Marqeta.

Buy Now, Pay Later (BNPL):  

- Split-payment services integrated with e-commerce.
- Examples: Klarna, Afterpay, Affirm.

Open Banking:  

- Mandated sharing of financial data with third parties through APIs.
- Examples: Plaid (U.S.), TrueLayer (Europe).

### 8. Financial Middleware
Bridges legacy systems with modern fintech solutions.

API Gateways:  

- Middleware to expose legacy banking services to external applications.
- Examples: MuleSoft, Apigee.

Integration Platforms:  

- Connect core banking systems with third-party services.
- Examples: WSO2, Dell Boomi.


### 9. Physical and Digital Cash Infrastructure
The backbone for physical and digital money flow.

ATM Networks: Physical cash distribution systems.  

- Examples: Cirrus, Plus, Link.

Digital Wallets:  

- Store and manage digital currency for online and offline use.
- Examples: Paytm, Alipay, Venmo.

### Who Owns and Maintains This Infrastructure?
- Global Corporations: Visa, Mastercard, SWIFT, AWS, Google Cloud.
- Financial Institutions: JPMorgan Chase, HSBC, Bank of America.
- Tech Companies: Stripe, PayPal, Square.
- Blockchain Foundations: Ethereum Foundation, Bitcoin Core developers.
- Government Entities: Central banks, regulatory authorities (e.g., Federal Reserve, ECB).
- Fintech Startups: Revolut, Wise, Plaid.

### Who Uses This Infrastructure?
- Banks and Financial Institutions: To process payments, manage risk, and comply with regulations.
- Fintech Companies: To deliver innovative solutions for payments, lending, and investment.
- Businesses: For embedded financial services, treasury management, and payroll.
- Consumers: For digital banking, remittances, and online payments.
- Governments and Regulators: For financial oversight, regulation, and monetary policy.
