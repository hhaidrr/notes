# Workflows of the Financial Services Industry

## Banking

* Account Opening:
    * Customer KYC (Know Your Customer): Verification of the customer’s identity.
    * Account Setup: Creation of a new account in the bank's system.
* Deposit Processing:
    * Handling deposits through ATMs, checks, wire transfers, and direct deposits.
* Loan Origination and Underwriting:
    * Application: The customer applies for a loan.
    * Credit Evaluation: The bank evaluates creditworthiness using credit scores, income verification, and other criteria.
    * Loan Approval/Denial: Decision is made based on underwriting.
    * Disbursement: The loan is disbursed to the borrower.
* Payments and Transfers:
    * Fund Transfer: Processing payments for bills, peer-to-peer transfers, and interbank transfers.
    * International Payments: Cross-border payments involving currency conversion, international wire transfers, etc.
* Account Maintenance:
    * Updating account details, handling account closure requests, and resolving issues.

## Investing

* Client Onboarding:
    * Risk Profiling: Understanding the client's risk tolerance.
    * Investment Goals Setup: Defining investment objectives, such as retirement planning or wealth accumulation.
* Portfolio Management:
    * Asset Allocation: Deciding how to distribute investments among different asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate, etc.).
    * Portfolio Monitoring: Regular tracking and rebalancing of investments.
* Trade Execution:
    * Buying and selling securities (stocks, bonds, etc.) based on portfolio strategies.
* Performance Reporting:
    * Regular reporting on the portfolio’s performance relative to benchmarks, including asset appreciation or depreciation.

## Lending

* Loan Application:
    * Gathering borrower information (income, credit history, etc.).
* Loan Evaluation and Underwriting:
    * Credit check, risk assessment, and determining loan terms (interest rate, repayment schedule, etc.).
* Loan Approval:
    * Final decision to approve or reject the loan.
* Loan Disbursement:
    * Transferring the loan amount to the borrower.
* Repayment Tracking:
    * Managing and tracking monthly payments, interest, and loan term compliance.
* Loan Closure:
    * Final payment and closure of the loan account after all obligations are met.

## Business Finance Workflows
Each of these look distinctly different and vary by vertical offering natural differentiation.
### Accounts Receivable (AR)
Purpose: Managing inbound payments from customers for goods or services.
Key Features:
- Invoicing customers.
- Tracking overdue payments.
- Reconciliation of received payments.
- Managing disputes and deductions.
Critical for: Ensuring steady cash inflow and minimizing overdue payments.
### Accounts Payable (AP)
Purpose: Managing outbound payments to vendors, suppliers, and contractors.
Key Features:
- Vendor invoice processing and approvals.
- Managing early payment discounts.
- Ensuring timely payments to avoid penalties.
- Fraud prevention in vendor payments.
Critical for: Maintaining supplier relationships and smooth operations.
### Payroll
Purpose: Paying employees, contractors, and related benefits.
Key Features:
- Salary calculation, including taxes, deductions, and bonuses.
- Disbursing payments via direct deposit or checks.
- Compliance with tax and labor laws.
- Handling benefits and reimbursements.
Critical for: Ensuring employee satisfaction and compliance.
### Treasury Management
Purpose: Managing the business’s overall liquidity and financial strategy.
Key Features:
- Cash flow forecasting and optimization.
- Monitoring bank balances and transactions.
- Managing investments and financing activities.
- Interbank transfers and debt repayments.
Critical for: Ensuring liquidity to meet operational and strategic needs.
### Cross-border Payments
Purpose: Handling payments to and from international clients, vendors, and employees.
Key Features:
- Currency conversion and foreign exchange rate management.
- Ensuring compliance with international regulations (e.g., AML, FATCA).
- Reducing transaction costs and delays.
- Managing global tax implications.
Critical for: Businesses with international operations or partnerships.
