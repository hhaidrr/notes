# Workflows of the Financial Services Industry

## Banking

* Account Opening:
    * Customer KYC (Know Your Customer): Verification of the customer’s identity.
    * Account Setup: Creation of a new account in the bank's system.
* Deposit Processing:
    * Handling deposits through ATMs, checks, wire transfers, and direct deposits.
* Loan Origination and Underwriting:
    * Application: The customer applies for a loan.
    * Credit Evaluation: The bank evaluates creditworthiness using credit scores, income verification, and other criteria.
    * Loan Approval/Denial: Decision is made based on underwriting.
    * Disbursement: The loan is disbursed to the borrower.
* Payments and Transfers:
    * Fund Transfer: Processing payments for bills, peer-to-peer transfers, and interbank transfers.
    * International Payments: Cross-border payments involving currency conversion, international wire transfers, etc.
* Account Maintenance:
    * Updating account details, handling account closure requests, and resolving issues.

## Investing

* Client Onboarding:
    * Risk Profiling: Understanding the client's risk tolerance.
    * Investment Goals Setup: Defining investment objectives, such as retirement planning or wealth accumulation.
* Portfolio Management:
    * Asset Allocation: Deciding how to distribute investments among different asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate, etc.).
    * Portfolio Monitoring: Regular tracking and rebalancing of investments.
* Trade Execution:
    * Buying and selling securities (stocks, bonds, etc.) based on portfolio strategies.
* Performance Reporting:
    * Regular reporting on the portfolio’s performance relative to benchmarks, including asset appreciation or depreciation.

## Lending

* Loan Application:
    * Gathering borrower information (income, credit history, etc.).
* Loan Evaluation and Underwriting:
    * Credit check, risk assessment, and determining loan terms (interest rate, repayment schedule, etc.).
* Loan Approval:
    * Final decision to approve or reject the loan.
* Loan Disbursement:
    * Transferring the loan amount to the borrower.
* Repayment Tracking:
    * Managing and tracking monthly payments, interest, and loan term compliance.
* Loan Closure:
    * Final payment and closure of the loan account after all obligations are met.
