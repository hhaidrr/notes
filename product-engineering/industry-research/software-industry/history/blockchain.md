# Blockchain

Blockchain is a very new concept. At the surface with my very beginner view on it, seems like a 
general way of maintaining a decentralized integral ledger.

It was only really applied to cryptocurrencies initially, however we can use it's general 
advantages for many use-cases

Governments and central banks began exploring blockchain for digital currencies (e.g., China's Digital Yuan).

Decentralized Finance (DeFi) platforms flourished, creating new financial models.

Use cases expanded to:
- Supply Chain: Companies like Walmart tracked goods using blockchain.
- Digital Identity: Blockchain was applied to secure identity verification.
- NFTs: Non-fungible tokens gained popularity, using blockchain for digital ownership.

- Blockchain became integral to technologies like Web3, emphasizing decentralization.
- Governments, including the EU and the US, began drafting comprehensive blockchain regulations.
- Corporate Integration:
- Large companies (e.g., Visa, Mastercard, Tesla) integrated blockchain for payments or asset management.
- Industries like real estate and logistics adopted blockchain for transparency and efficiency.
- Public awareness increased with the growth of NFTs, metaverse projects, and decentralized apps.
Barriers: Scalability, regulatory uncertainty, and energy concerns slowed adoption in some areas.
