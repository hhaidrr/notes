# Case Studies & Transformative Experiences
Case studies in this regard refer to exploring new ways of doing things to find emperic results the brain can 
evolve from.

Doing things the same way again and again conditions the brain's sense of realistic capabilities and limitations to 
match the constraints of the status quo. Our perception of what is actually possible is defined by our brain based 
on what it has seen physically.

The purpose of case studies are a planned way of getting our brain exposed to new things. The results of these 
experiments allow our brain to become well educated and calibrate it for what is actually possible with what's out there.

When the brain does this, it transforms it on a fundamental level allowing it to pursue things it previously thought
were impossible or not worth the investment.

e.g. 
- Becoming an entrepreneur seeming like an unrealistic goal to someone who has worked a stable job their whole life.
They never even try it so they never gain the physical reward of that pursuit. 

- A classical coder who has never used AI tools their whole career never gains the productivity boost AI tools have to 
offer and never integrates them with their own workflow.
