# Units of Measure

What are the units of measures in product engineering?

Well we know the value equation deals with:
- time
- capital
- physical energy
- risk

So we can start with these as our primitive types.

## Time
We already have developed and established units of measure for time.

## Capital
We already have developed units of measure for capital.

## Energy
We have units for measure for purely kinetic forms of energy thanks to physics.
However, many products to day don't require much physical energy to operate.
We operate huge machines and complex systems through buttons, panels, switches, etc. 
Either analog or digital, static, or dynamic, we operate systems through *interfaces*.

Interfaces are systems in of themselves. Tasked with the function of conveying the 
state our system captures while conveying our intent back to it to perform functions.

Interfaces deserve its own topic document. However the key metric that is not often measured
is cognitive energy.

A certain amount of cognitive energy is required to use a system. I feel it is the 
interface design of a system which has the most influence over how much cognitive effort 
is required to operate it.
