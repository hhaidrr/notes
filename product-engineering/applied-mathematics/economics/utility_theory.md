# Utility Theory

There are many different utility formulae and theories. However they all differ from the original
value theory defined in our value framework. 

Traditional utility theory is very tied to:
- qualitative features of commodities
    - e.g. 2 apples vs 3 oranges
- Predicting preference between different outcomes
    - e.g. will the actor prefer outcome A (2 apples, 3 oranges) or outcome B (3 apples, 3 oranges)
        
Our value framework and formulae do not try to predict preference of desired outcome state.
We make the broader acceptance of the assumption that a consumer's preference is complex to the
point of being arbitrary.

However, we know that there are variables which influence preference besides what a product can do
and shift what is considered important to how efficiently a product achieves it's target outcome.
Using heaven theory as the ideal state, deriving cost of achieving utilization as energy primitives
such as time, capital, and risk. Every utility algorithm which can be a series of produces/services
in use to achieve the same outcome will be objectively preferred based on these energy primitives
given that both sets of algorithms achieve the same arbitrary outcome.

It then becomes a matter of the path of least resistance, or shortest path algorithm.

All cardinal desires of the actor/human come from the human model algorithms of 
perception(external state) and homeostasis()


The issue that may arise from our framework is that products typically do different things, 
and thus have pros and cons, which are covered by the use of option theory,
to create a superset of mass utility/capability which satisfy the superset of combined 
actor arbitrary desired outcomes.
