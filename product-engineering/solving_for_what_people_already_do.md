# Solving for what people already do

People adopt products that make what they already do (outcomes) easier, faster, cheaper, or safer.

Its hard to get people to do new things. It's more sure that people will use tools to aid them in doing what they already do.

To build a good tool or service for what people do, you must know a lot about their current process. This is why solving your own problem is common formula for founders. Because there is no knowledge gap between
the founder and the problem. The founder is the user.

This can be seen as a formula for product building. So how do we apply it to other people's use cases which we have no experience with?

We have to have a process of bridging the knowledge gap.
- What other things to people do?
- What are the steps they take to do it?
- What are the tools they use?

This is known as a value chain, it is taught in business schools. [First Lesson Taught in Harvard MBA in 18 Minutes | Thales Teixeira](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UA_gYKaXHS0&t=946s)

Then, once you have a solid understanding of another user's value chain, you can start product building (mvp, iterate with user feedback, etc).
However, unlike building for yourself, this is more difficult and nuanced since you have to partner with users to constantly get accurate empirical feedback on the value of your product. 
When you build for yourself, this overhead does not exist at the same magnitude.
