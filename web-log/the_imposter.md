# The imposter

2025-10-10

Its june or july of year three of my career, something changed, a turning point. The color of my lenses inverted.

Like someone disconnected the plug to my source of energy. Panic attacks during standups, physically not being able to speak due to the chest clinching anxiety I hadn't felt in years suddenly rushing to every part of me that used to feel calm.

I don't know if the disconnect came first or the anxiety. Which one was the cause and which were the symptom. Though now in october, things have not gone back to the way they were.

The anxiety is better but not gone. Every notification from work sends a tremor of angst through my body. Where before it would be exhilirating. 

I started this job thinking I had much to offer, that I was above average in intelligence, ambition, and results. I did well, I got far, and indeed I was titled to be a senior.

I held the role of senior engineer, and cybersecurity lead, yet I was still green. Or so I felt. My skills carried the product to where it is today.

Though now the dynamics have changed. More experienced engineers have joined the team in person, while I remain remote. Where I was once the source of truth for many things. 

I now feel like I have to catch up with things I don't know about. I know in the back of my head that this was the natural path of evolution to scale the team. More talent, more distributed work and context.

Though it pains even more when the team get to solve things together in person where I sit isolated in my chair across the country. 

I don't feel like the sharpest sword in the belt anymore. That was my identity for 2 and a half years. I felt proud, I felt confident, I felt ownership and passion to push the organization.

Now I feel no pride, I feel doubtful of myself, I don't feel ownership over anything besides the code I write for the ticket i'm assigned. 

Worse on the security side since the beginning. I was only a student of cybersecurity with no real world experience, to be named the lead, well I had no idea what I was doing (I had a bit) and still I don't even 

have time to fulfull this obligation.

Things are different, and now i'm realizing that's okay. I always heard about imposter syndrome and it seems intangible, I couldn't empathize with it because I didn't really know how it could be a real thing.

Now I know how real it is. That what I had once is all gone, and everything is now gray.

The reality is my skills have gotten better since I first started, yet I feel the opposite of myself, how strange. 

Maybe its because of how i'm comparing myself, we now have talent that outshines me. People that have become sources of context besides me, I have somewhat lost my previous identity. 

I question my worth in the company, where I used to feel my worth was unquestionable.

Truth is, my disconection started with seeds of pursuing my own independence. I started realizing my undying passion for the organization got me raises and reputation but they did not care about my own bottom line.

No organization does. That if I really wanted the best for myself, it wasnt going to come from making my employers happy. This remains absolutely true and fact.

So the disconnection is natural. Maybe that's what came first then. 

Feeling and knowing what I do now, I don't think I will ever feel the same for the company. The team will grow and disconnect further. It was always meant to go this direction. It was inevitable.

I have to learn to be okay with this.

I have to double down on my own path.

My objective before was to be the best engineer at the company, be seen and valued the most by the stakeholders. I acheived that, indeed I did.

Though now I have a new objective, to serve the market directly. For this, I had to disconnect even from engineering. I had to look at a layer higher and earn my stripes in a new arena.

The arena of business and market production. Engineering can be outsourced, there will always be someone better than me, I am not the smartest man in the room, nor do I think I will be. To think this is ironically not smart. So maybe I'm smarter than I think. Though to achieve outcome is to know yourself and know others, know the environment.

This chapter marks something new. I need to walk along with my new found anxiety that I am not the golden goose I once was, though embrace it while pursuing my new objective. To serve the market in my own way. To learn the ways of business, like I always wanted. That was my want for myself not anyone else. I wanted to be the best engineer for my employer, to be valued the most, have the most esteem. Now I want independence. Such a unremarkable want, so common, so typical of a person. Though a want nonetheless, a set of skills to aquire, and not an easy feat by any means. Which makes it an objective worth pursuing. 

An objective for myself, not for anyone else.

Business involves engineering too though. I like making things. It involves code sometimes, though ultimately it involves human psychology, manufacturing, production, distribution, human resources, so much more.

Business like any skill in the wild is something I now consider a survival skill. Business and entrepreneurship are ancient skills. To produce, and to sell, is a fundamental skill to survive. Those that learn it can live off the human market without an intermediary. Hence the inherit independence. Just like the skills of making a fire, shelter, hunting food in a forest are essential skills that allow you to sustain yourself and make you independent. Business allows you to survive off the human market of commerce. It is the new essentiall skill I want to learn. Programming is a niche skill that is further from survival.

It requires an employer that will pay you for that skill in order for you to survive. Your survival and well being are on the employer. Business is timeless, no employer is needed. It always requires 
product market fit, always requires distribution.

You can't know everything, but you better believe you can learn anything. Because you can.
