# Measuring the Real World

How do we quantify real world phenomena? This task is a prerequisite to actually making 
systems that will operate on top of such phenomena. Without metrics, there is no "system".

For a system to act deterministically, predictably and consistently, it must operate 
on concrete measures.

## Meters
I am using the term "meter" to describe a tool which has the capability to measure the 
world. Meters are typically the tools used to make measurements for any given field of 
study. 

Physics and chemistry are two major examples of fields that use various kinds of meters to
measure what was once seemingly unquantifiable phenomena. Thinking about quantifying the 
units of measure within these fields before they were established are just as mystifying
as it seems now to measure whatever units would be relevant to product engineering.

This is a problem I would often think about and be stumped. However I now see clearly that
the systems which provide measurements are in a completely different category than the 
systems which are built on top of them. Another way to look at it is, there are systems
which take in raw sensory input from the world and output our defined metrics. While there
are systems which take in our defined metrics as input and output our engineered output.
These two categories are completely independent. Yet the latter cannot exist without the 
former.

So with this new clarity. We will need to develop meters first, before we can develop 
compounding systems.
