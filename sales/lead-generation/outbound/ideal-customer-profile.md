* Decision Maker:	(CFO, CEO, Head of AP, Controller)
* Pain Points:	
        - Delays in Payment Processing: Cheque processing is slow, often causing significant delays in both incoming and outgoing payments. This can lead to disruptions in cash flow and make it harder for businesses to manage their finances effectively.

        - Cash Flow Management Issues: Because cheques take time to clear, businesses may face overdraft risks and struggle to meet payment obligations to suppliers or employees on time, increasing financial stress.

        - High Risk of Fraud and Security Concerns: Cheques are vulnerable to fraud, which remains a top concern for small businesses when handling payments.

        - Administrative Burden and Human Error: Manual handling of cheques increases the likelihood of mistakes (such as misplacing cheques or incorrect data entry), which can further delay processing and reconciliation.

        - Operational Inefficiencies: The need for manual intervention, physical handling, and bank visits adds time and cost, reducing overall efficiency.

        - Lack of Transparency and Tracking: Cheque-based payments make it difficult to track payment status and reconcile accounts quickly, leading to uncertainty and extra administrative work.
